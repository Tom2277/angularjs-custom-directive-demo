This page has no AngularJS on it.

It does not use the angular-ui router either, of course. That will cause angular-ui-router links ('ui-ref') to break. You will want a separate navbar with normal links to insert via JavaScript or cut and paste onto all of your non routed pages.

The router is great in terms of page reload time, and of course, it keeps you from generating separate pages with navbars and src="" references.

Sometimes, however, you -Do- want a fresh page reload when your content changes. If you're running site with advertising on it for example, your client may feel that they've earned a new page view when there is new content on the page?!

Or maybe a client is attached to a HTML image map they made in 2003? Or perhaps they have legacy pages with many back links to them that they want to maintain as landing and soft-redirecting pages?

It's simple to use AngularJS on some pages and not others is simple when a site is up on simple s3 bucket and/or HTTP server.

Of course, rich dynamic sites are rarely that simple.
