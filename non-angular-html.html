This page has no AngularJS on it.

Neither does the the handlebars sortable table demo page.

They do not use the angular-ui router either, of course. That will cause angular-ui-router links ('ui-ref') to break. You will want a separate navbar with normal links to insert via JavaScript or cut and paste onto all of your non routed pages.

A single-page app using a front-end frameworks router is great in terms of page reload time.

Sometimes, however, you -Do- want a fresh page reload. If you're running site with pay per view advertising, your client may feel that they've earned a new page view whenever new content is displayed on a page?!

Or perhaps the client has legacy pages with many back links to their urls that shouldn't be broken for SEO reaons?

It's simple to use AngularJS on some pages and not others with a site deployed on a simple s3 bucket and/or HTTP server.

Of course, rich dynamic sites are rarely that simple.
